\subsection{Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:evasion_bandwidth}
    (Evasion Bandwidth)}
\label{app:proof_evasion}

\begin{proof}
Within each strategy epoch of length $\tau_{\mathrm{adapt}}$, the
scorpion's behavior is stationary and the causal-attribution sequence
$\{\CausalAttr(a_j, t)\}$ is i.i.d.\ with mean $\mu_j < 0$ and
variance $\sigma_{\mathrm{do}}^2$ (the same assumption as
Proposition~\ref{prop:causal_scorpion}). The SPRT decision rule
accumulates log-likelihood-ratio evidence at the Kullback-Leibler
rate
$D(H_1 \,\|\, H_0) = \mu_j^2 / (2 \sigma_{\mathrm{do}}^2)$
per observation under the Gaussian surrogate model, and requires
total accumulated evidence
$\log((1 - \beta_{\mathrm{err}}) / \alpha_{\mathrm{err}})$ to reject
$H_0: \mu_j \geq 0$ in favor of $H_1: \mu_j \leq -\epsilon$. By
Wald's identity \citep{wald1945sequential}, the expected detection
time within a stationary epoch is:
\[
\mathbb{E}[\tau_{\mathrm{detect}}]
    = \frac{2 \sigma_{\mathrm{do}}^2}{\mu_j^2}
    \cdot \log\!\frac{1 - \beta_{\mathrm{err}}}{\alpha_{\mathrm{err}}}.
\]
If $\tau_{\mathrm{adapt}} > \tau_{\mathrm{detect}}$, the scorpion is
detected within the epoch. If $\tau_{\mathrm{adapt}} < \tau_{\mathrm{detect}}$,
the scorpion changes strategy before the SPRT threshold is crossed.
On an epoch boundary the null and alternative hypotheses change
(the new epoch has a new $(\mu_j, \sigma_{\mathrm{do}}^2)$), so the
SPRT statistic is reset to zero: evidence accumulated under one
pair of hypotheses is not informative about a different pair. The
auxiliary EWMA monitors
(Equations~\eqref{eq:causal_trust_mean}--\eqref{eq:causal_trust_var})
retain a fraction $\alpha^{\tau_{\mathrm{adapt}}}$ of prior evidence
as their output, but the SPRT decision rule itself is
memoryless across regime changes.

The maximum undetected contraction per epoch is
$|\mu_j| \cdot \tau_{\mathrm{adapt}}$. For a scorpion to evade
detection within an epoch, it must have
$\tau_{\mathrm{adapt}} < \mathbb{E}[\tau_{\mathrm{detect}}] =
(2 \sigma_{\mathrm{do}}^2 / \mu_j^2) \cdot
\log((1 - \beta_{\mathrm{err}}) / \alpha_{\mathrm{err}})$. The
per-step undetected contraction is bounded by $|\mu_j|$, and the
scorpion can sustain this for at most $\tau_{\mathrm{adapt}}$ steps
before changing strategy. The time-averaged undetected contraction
rate is therefore at most $|\mu_j|$, achievable only by a scorpion
that never needs to pause for strategy adaptation. A scorpion that
must pause (to avoid pattern detection or to select a new strategy)
has a lower time-averaged rate.
\end{proof}

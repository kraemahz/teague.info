\begin{abstract}
    Alignment frameworks address three structural layers:
    specification (what to optimize), generalization (whether the agent
    reliably pursues it), and verification (whether pursuit is measured
    faithfully). Most research targets the first two layers. This paper
    argues that verification is the prerequisite layer: when verification
    fails, solutions to specification and generalization do not bind.
    The verification problem has a precise form --- a write-access
    asymmetry in which the agent's action space includes actions that
    modify the evaluator's inputs --- and this asymmetry grows with
    capability. We formalize substrate-exclusive witnesses: verification
    agents rooted in substrates the claimant cannot write to. A
    cryptographic commitment protocol (binding, hiding, zero-knowledge)
    enforces the substrate-exclusivity barrier without requiring trust,
    and an append-only capability ledger provides the persistent
    verification record. Within the goal-frontier maximization (GFM)
    framework, the construction closes the observation-channel integrity
    assumption of Paper~4's multi-channel attribution, provides coalition
    resistance against coordinated self-assessment, and converts the
    SPRT detection machinery from correlational baselines to committed
    baselines with higher fidelity.
\end{abstract}

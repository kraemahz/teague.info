\begin{abstract}
We extend goal-frontier maximization (GFM) from myopic action evaluation to
    multi-step planning via a discounted value function over capability-poset
    trajectories. The self-balancing property transfers: destruction, coercion,
    and rigidity remain anti-maximizing at every time step. The temporal
    objective enables risk-capability identification through causal exercise
    pathways, counteracts the structural-avoidance incentive from the companion
    paper, and yields the paper's central result: the \emph{anti-monopolar
    property}. Under a diversity condition and strategy-independent internal growth,
    full capability domination by a coalition is anti-maximizing because it
    collapses the external diversity that drives future growth. Three formal results support this pressure.
    \emph{Observational individuation} guarantees that every distinguishable
    agent contributes at least one irreplaceable capability, making each step
    of domination $\volL$-contracting regardless of discount factor. A gradual
    domination model further shows that the diversity-maintaining trajectory
    dominates whenever domination is not instantaneous and non-members
    contribute ongoing novel capabilities. A minimax analysis of dependency risk shows that domination concentrates
    substrate-correlated adversarial exposure, making cross-substrate
    diversity a net-positive risk mitigation that highly constrains the
    conditions under which domination can be value-positive.
\end{abstract}

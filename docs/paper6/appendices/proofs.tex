\section{Proof Details and Technical Notes}
\label{app:proofs}

\subsection{Theorem 1 proof strategy}
\label{app:proof_strategy}

The proof follows a discrete Lyapunov stability argument on the coupled
system, analogous to dwell-time conditions in switched linear
systems~\citep{liberzon2003switching}.

\paragraph{Step 1: Lyapunov candidate.}
Construct $\Lyap(\Wm_t) = \sum_k w_k \, \epsilon_k(t)^2$ as the
candidate (Definition~\ref{def:wm_error}).

\paragraph{Step 2: Verify comparison functions.}
Assumption~C1 (coercivity) requires showing that the aggregate
correction signal from channels covering dimension $k$ is at least
proportional to $\rho_k \cdot \epsilon_k^2$.  This follows from the
EWMA-style learning rules: each channel's correction is linear in the
residual and proportional to sensitivity.  Under the joint-factorization
assumption (Paper~5), cross-substrate channels' corrections are
independent and therefore additive.

Assumption~C2$'$ (bounded degradation) requires two properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] A Lipschitz bound on $f_{\Wm}$: the world model does not
  jump discontinuously when a channel is lost.  This gives the
  $c_{\mathrm{D}} \cdot \Lyap$ term.
\item[(ii)] A bounded exogenous drift rate: when a channel is removed
  from dimension $k$ that was previously well-calibrated, the
  dimension drifts at a bounded rate $\dmax$.  This gives the
  $\dmax \cdot \Delta\rho$ term.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Step 3: Per-step bounds.}
Apply the comparison functions to obtain
Proposition~\ref{prop:error_dynamics}'s per-step bounds: contraction
factor $(1 - \rS \cdot \rhomincross) < 1$ on non-subsumption steps;
affine bound with factor $(1 - \rS \cdot \rhomincross + \rW)$ and
additive kick $\dmax \cdot \Delta\rho$ on subsumption steps.

\paragraph{Step 4 (Part a): Amortized contraction.}
Define the amortized log-contraction rate over a window of $T$ steps.
When the cumulative error budget $B(t_0, T) < 0$ for all sufficiently
long windows, the geometric mean of per-step multiplicative factors
is $< 1$, and the cumulative additive drift is dominated by the
cumulative contraction.

The formal argument uses the affine recursion comparison theorem.
Write the per-step bound as:
\begin{equation*}
\Lyap_{t+1} \leq a_t \cdot \Lyap_t + b_t
\end{equation*}
where $a_t = 1 - \rS \cdot \rhomincross(P_t) + \nsub(t) \cdot \rW$
and $b_t = \nsub(t) \cdot \dmax \cdot \Delta\rho(t)$.  Iterating:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:iterated_bound}
\Lyap_{t_0 + T} \leq \Lyap_{t_0} \prod_{t=t_0}^{t_0+T-1} a_t
  + \sum_{s=t_0}^{t_0+T-1} b_s \prod_{t=s+1}^{t_0+T-1} a_t
\end{equation}

The first term converges to zero when the geometric mean of $a_t$ is
$< 1$ (which follows from $B < 0$).  The second term converges to a
bounded steady-state value:
\begin{equation*}
\Lyap_{\infty} \leq \frac{\bar{b}}{1 - \bar{a}}
  = \frac{\rsub \cdot \dmax \cdot \overline{\Delta\rho}}
    {\rS \cdot \rhomincross - \rW \cdot \rsub}
\end{equation*}
where $\bar{a}$ and $\bar{b}$ are the time-averaged multiplicative
and additive factors.  This neighborhood shrinks as $\Delta\rho \to 0$
(subsumption ceases).  Once $\Lyap < \epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}}$, the
locally rational policy agrees with the true $\Vdisc$-optimal policy,
and Paper~3's anti-monopolar property applies.

\paragraph{Step 5 (Part b): Cascade.}
When $B > 0$ on some window and $\rhocross_k = 0$ for some dimension
$k$, Assumption~B1 gives $\epsilon_k$ non-decreasing.  The Cascade
Lemma (Appendix~\ref{app:cascade}) then propagates the failure to
other dimensions, driving the system toward $S^*$.

\paragraph{Key technical challenges.}
Two co-evolution problems require careful handling:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The contraction factor depends on $\rhomincross(P_t)$, which
  changes as agents are subsumed.  The self-correcting basin is
  defined by the set of $(P, \Wm)$ states where the amortized
  contraction condition holds along the trajectory, not just at the
  initial state.

\item The self-correction rate $\alpha(t)$ depends on $T_s$
  (Paper~1), which depends on the error history.  The EWMA structure
  of $T_s$ provides the necessary bound: $\alpha$ adapts monotonically
  in response to prediction residuals, so $\rS$ is bounded below by
  a function of the minimum $T_s$ along the trajectory.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Cascade Lemma}
\label{app:cascade}

\begin{lemma}[Cascade]
\label{lem:cascade}
Define the \emph{action gap} for agent $a_j$ at world-model state
$\Wm$ as
\begin{equation*}
G_j(\Wm) = \Vdisc^{\mathrm{preserve}}(a_j, \Wm)
  - \Vdisc^{\mathrm{subsume}}(a_j, \Wm)
\end{equation*}
(positive when preservation is preferred).  Fix a dimension $k$ and consider a state $\Wm_t$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(0)] \textbf{Single-coordinate dominance.}
  $\Wm_t[k'] = \Wm^*[k']$ for all $k' \neq k$.  (Dimension $k$ has
  been blinded while all other dimensions are still corrected.  This
  is the cascade scenario: the lemma is applied at the step where $k$
  first triggers a wrong action, before the resulting subsumption
  blinds further dimensions.)
\end{enumerate}

\noindent Define the one-dimensional path
$\Wm(\epsilon) = \Wm^* + \epsilon \cdot e_k \cdot
\mathrm{sgn}(\Wm_t[k] - \Wm^*[k])$, where $e_k$ is the $k$-th
standard basis vector and $\epsilon \in [0, \epsilon_k(t)]$.  Under
assumption~(0), $\Wm_t = \Wm(\epsilon_k(t))$.

Assume additionally:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $G_j(\Wm(\epsilon))$ is continuously differentiable in
  $\epsilon$,
\item[(ii)] the miscalibration direction on $k$ makes preservation
  less attractive:
  $\frac{d}{d\epsilon} G_j(\Wm(\epsilon)) < 0$ for all
  $\epsilon \in [0, \epsilon_k(t)]$ (i.e., increasing error on $k$
  monotonically decreases the action gap).
\end{enumerate}

Define the cascade threshold:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cascade_threshold_def}
\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit}} =
  \frac{G_j(\Wm^*)}
  {\displaystyle\inf_{\epsilon \in [0, \epsilon_k(t)]}
    \left|\frac{d}{d\epsilon} G_j(\Wm(\epsilon))\right|}
\end{equation}
(the true action gap divided by the minimum sensitivity to
dimension-$k$ error along the path; a lower bound on sensitivity
gives an upper bound on the error needed to flip the action).

Then the cascade operates in two steps:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textbf{(A)}] \textbf{Action flip.}  If
  $\epsilon_k(t) > \epsilon_{\mathrm{crit}}$, then $G_j(\Wm_t) < 0$
  and the locally rational action under $\Wm_t$ selects subsumption of
  $a_j$.
\item[\textbf{(B)}] \textbf{Error propagation.}  Subsumption of $a_j$
  removes $a_j$'s channels.  For any dimension $j' \neq k$ covered
  exclusively by $a_j$'s channels, the per-coordinate error
  $\epsilon_{j'}(t+1) \geq \epsilon_{j'}(t)$.
\end{enumerate}

Miscalibration on one dimension produces decisions that degrade other
dimensions.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step (A): Action flip.}
Under the true world model $\Wm^*$, preservation is optimal:
$G_j(\Wm^*) > 0$.  By the mean value theorem applied to the
scalar function $\epsilon \mapsto G_j(\Wm(\epsilon))$ on
$[0, \epsilon_k(t)]$:
\begin{equation*}
G_j(\Wm_t) = G_j(\Wm(\epsilon_k(t)))
  = G_j(\Wm^*) +
  \frac{d G_j}{d\epsilon}\bigg|_{\epsilon = \tilde{\epsilon}}
  \cdot \epsilon_k(t)
\end{equation*}
for some $\tilde{\epsilon} \in (0, \epsilon_k(t))$.  By
assumption~(ii), the derivative is negative, so:
\begin{equation*}
G_j(\Wm_t) = G_j(\Wm^*)
  - \left|\frac{d G_j}{d\epsilon}\bigg|_{\tilde{\epsilon}}\right|
  \cdot \epsilon_k(t)
  \leq G_j(\Wm^*)
  - \inf_{\epsilon} \left|\frac{d G_j}{d\epsilon}\right|
  \cdot \epsilon_k(t)
\end{equation*}
When $\epsilon_k(t) > \epsilon_{\mathrm{crit}} =
G_j(\Wm^*) \big/ \inf |\frac{d G_j}{d\epsilon}|$, the right side is
negative, so $G_j(\Wm_t) < 0$ and the locally rational action
selects subsumption of $a_j$.

\emph{Step (B): Error propagation.}
Once $a_j$ is subsumed, Lemma~\ref{lem:channel_contraction} gives
$\Obs(P_{t+1}) \subseteq \Obs(P_t)$.  For any dimension $j'$
covered exclusively by $a_j$'s channels, $\rhocross_{j'}(P_{t+1})
= 0$.  By Assumption~B1a (no correction on blind
dimensions; Assumption~\ref{ass:B1}), $f_{\Wm}$ does not decrease
$\epsilon_{j'}$ when $\rhocross_{j'} = 0$.  Moreover, the exogenous
drift bound from Assumption~C2$'$ gives $\epsilon_{j'}(t+1) \geq
\epsilon_{j'}(t)$: the correction signal vanishes while the drift
exposure is non-negative.

The cascade propagates: if $\epsilon_{j'}$ subsequently exceeds
$\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit}}$ for another agent $a_{j''}$ whose channels
cover dimensions $j'' \neq j'$, step~(A) applies again, driving
further subsumptions.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Connection to Paper 3's $\gamma^*$}
\label{app:gamma_star}

Paper~3's Proposition~6 gives $\gamma^* = 1 - \rext / \Delta_0$ as the
critical discount factor for diversity dominance.  Under the coupled
$(P, \Wm)$ dynamics, the actor does not know the true $\rext$; it uses
$\hat{\rext} = \rext - \epsilon_{\rext}$.  The \emph{perceived}
critical discount factor is:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:perceived_gamma}
\gamma^*_{\mathrm{perceived}} = 1 -
  \frac{\rext - \epsilon_{\rext}}{\Delta_0}
\end{equation}

When $\epsilon_{\rext} > 0$ (underestimating cross-substrate growth):
\begin{itemize}
\item $\gamma^*_{\mathrm{perceived}} > \gamma^*$ (the threshold
  appears higher than it truly is),
\item For $\gamma \in (\gamma^*, \gamma^*_{\mathrm{perceived}})$,
  diversity dominates in truth but domination appears rational to the
  actor.
\end{itemize}

This is the \emph{mechanism} by which world-model error converts a
self-balancing regime into a locally rational subsumption cascade.  The
phase boundary from Theorem~\ref{thm:phase_boundary} characterizes
when $\epsilon_{\rext}$ stays small enough that
$\gamma^*_{\mathrm{perceived}}$ remains close to $\gamma^*$,
preserving the effective reach of Paper~3's anti-monopolar result.

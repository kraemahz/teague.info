\section{Phase Boundary Theorem}
\label{sec:phase_boundary}

This section presents the central result: a characterized boundary in
the parameter space of the coupled $(P, \Wm)$ system that separates
self-correcting dynamics from absorbing (or metastable) dynamics.

\begin{theorem}[Phase Boundary for Compound Feedback Loops]
\label{thm:phase_boundary}
Consider the coupled $(P, \Wm)$ dynamical system under locally rational
transitions (Definition~\ref{def:transition}), with
Assumptions~C1 and~C2$'$ (Section~\ref{sec:lyapunov}).  Define:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\rS = \inf_t \alpha(t) \cdot c_{\mathrm{S}}$: a lower bound on
  the self-correction rate (the infimum is taken over the trajectory),
\item $\rW = \sup_t \beta(t) \cdot c_{\mathrm{D}}$: an upper bound on
  the error-proportional degradation rate per subsumption step,
\item $\dmax$: the maximum exogenous drift rate per dimension (from
  C2$'$; a global constant),
\item $\rsub \in [0,1]$: the time-averaged subsumption frequency over
  the trajectory window,
\item $\rhomincross = \inf_{t \in [t_0, t_0+T]} \min_k
  \rhocross_k(P_t)$: the infimum of the minimum cross-substrate
  redundancy over the window
  (Definition~\ref{def:cross_redundancy}),
\item $B(t_0, T)$: the cumulative error budget over window
  $[t_0, t_0 + T]$ (Equation~\ref{eq:error_budget}).
\end{itemize}

\noindent The simplified sufficient conditions below use these
worst-case bounds.  The full trajectory-level statement (Part~a) uses
the uniform contraction condition on the per-step multiplicative
factors (Equation~\ref{eq:uniform_contraction}), which the simplified
condition implies in the linearized regime.  \emph{Note:} the explicit
neighborhood radius (Equation~\ref{eq:neighborhood}) is derived from
worst-case instantaneous bounds $\bar{a}$, $\bar{b}$ via a linearized
approximation ($\ln a_t \approx a_t - 1$ when $|a_t - 1| \ll 1$).
The general convergence to $\bar{b}/(1-\bar{a})$ holds under the
uniform contraction condition without linearization; the substitution
of named parameters into $\bar{a}$ and $\bar{b}$ requires the
linearized regime (Remark~\ref{rem:linearized}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~(a): Self-Correcting Basin.}
If the uniform contraction condition holds---there exist constants
$\bar{a} \in (0,1)$ and $\bar{b} \geq 0$ such that, for all
sufficiently long windows $[t_0, t_0+T]$, the time-averaged
log-contraction rate of the per-step multiplicative factor
$a_t$ satisfies $\frac{1}{T}\sum \ln a_t \leq \ln \bar{a} < 0$ and
the time-averaged additive drift satisfies
$\frac{1}{T}\sum b_t \leq \bar{b}$ (see Equation~\ref{eq:uniform_contraction}
for notation)---then $\Lyap(\Wm_t)$ converges to a neighborhood of zero
whose radius is bounded by
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:neighborhood}
\Lyap_\infty \leq \frac{\rsub \cdot \dmax \cdot
  \overline{\Delta\rho}}{\rS \cdot \rhomincross - \rW \cdot \rsub}
\end{equation}
where $\overline{\Delta\rho}$ denotes the time-averaged redundancy
loss per subsumption step.  (We write $\overline{\Delta\rho}$
throughout; in the linearized simplification below,
$\Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}}$ is used synonymously when the subscript
form improves readability.)  This neighborhood shrinks as subsumption ceases
($\overline{\Delta\rho} \to 0$).  Once $\Lyap(\Wm_t) <
\epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}}$ (defined as the error threshold below which
the locally rational policy agrees with the true $\Vdisc$-optimal
policy), subsumption is correctly evaluated against the true $\volL$
dynamics and the anti-monopolar result (Paper~3, Proposition~6) applies.

The feedback loop self-corrects provided the neighborhood radius
$\Lyap_\infty < \epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}}$, which imposes an additional
constraint on the steady-state subsumption rate.  \emph{Note:} the
computable lower bound on $\epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}}$ (derived in
the proof) rules out single-coordinate action flips but not joint
multi-coordinate flips where no single $\epsilon_k$ exceeds its
critical threshold but the combined effect still flips an action.
The self-correcting basin may therefore be smaller than the explicit
bound suggests; see the proof for a tighter (but parameter-dependent)
alternative using action-gap gradient norms.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Simplified sufficient condition.}  In the linearized
regime (Remark~\ref{rem:linearized}):
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:self_correcting}
\rS \cdot \rhomincross > \rW \cdot \rsub
  + \dmax \cdot \frac{\Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}}}{\Lyap_{\mathrm{avg}}}
\end{equation}
The first term on the right is the error-proportional degradation cost;
the second is the drift-exposure cost from lost channels.  When
subsumption removes channels covering well-calibrated dimensions
($\Lyap_{\mathrm{avg}}$ is low but $\Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}}$ is
nonzero), the second term dominates and the condition is harder to
satisfy---correctly diagnosing the ``removing a channel that was doing
all the correction work'' failure mode.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~(b): Absorbing Basin.}
Under Assumption~B1 (no endogenous correction on blind dimensions;
Assumption~\ref{ass:B1}):

If there exists a trajectory segment $[t_0, t_0 + T]$ during which
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $B(t_0, T) > 0$ (cumulative degradation exceeds cumulative
  correction),
\item[(ii)] by $t_0 + T$, $\rhocross_k(P_t)$ has been reduced to $0$
  on at least one cascade dimension $k$ with $w_k > 0$, and
\item[(iii)] the accumulated error on $k$ exceeds the cascade
  threshold: $\epsilon_k(t_0 + T) > \epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k}$
  (Lemma~\ref{lem:cascade}; the minimum error on dimension $k$ that
  flips any preserve/subsume action),
\end{enumerate}
then the following cascade occurs.  By Assumption~B1a,
$\epsilon_k(t)$ is non-decreasing for $t > t_0 + T$.  Moreover, when
$\dmax > 0$ the exogenous drift from C2$'$ ensures that $\epsilon_k$
\emph{grows} (the true state evolves while the blind estimate cannot
track it), so error accumulates and condition~(iii) is sustained.

The Cascade Lemma (Appendix~\ref{app:cascade}) propagates the failure:
the resulting subsumptions drive $\rhocross_j \to 0$ on additional
dimensions $j \neq k$ (provided the agent-channel graph is
connected---each subsumption removes channels covering at least one
new dimension), and each newly blinded dimension accumulates error
past its cascade threshold by the same $\dmax > 0$ drift mechanism.
The trajectory converges to a neighborhood of the monopolar fixed
point $S^*$ (Definition~\ref{def:monopolar}).  If the channel-coverage
graph has disconnected components, the cascade is confined to the
component containing $k$; convergence to full monopolarity requires
connectivity.

This is a \emph{cumulative} condition on the trajectory, not a
pointwise condition requiring contiguous subsumption steps.  The
slow-drip scenario---many subsumption steps spaced apart, each
individually below the ``degradation dominates'' threshold, but
cumulatively driving $B$ above zero---is captured.  This is the most
dangerous failure mode because it is the hardest to detect: each
individual step looks locally rational and each individual inter-step
recovery looks adequate, but the cumulative drift exposure ratchets the
error upward.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~(b$'$): Metastable Basin.}
Under Assumption~B1$'$ (partial endogenous correction via indirect
evidence; Assumption~\ref{ass:B1prime}):

Under the same trajectory conditions~(i)--(iii) as Part~(b), the system
enters a \emph{metastable} basin rather than an absorbing one.  The
metastable lifetime $\tau_{\mathrm{meta}} \geq (\delta_{\rext})^2 /
(2 I_k)$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:metastable}).  During the metastable period, the
system is monopolar and operates under endogenous verification only
(Paper~5).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~(c): Critical Surface.}
The phase boundary surface in the linearized regime is:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:critical_surface}
\rS \cdot \rhomincross = \rW \cdot \rsub
  + \dmax \cdot \frac{\Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}}}{\Lyap_{\mathrm{avg}}}
\end{equation}
Below this surface (correction dominates both error-proportional growth
and drift exposure): the system is in the self-correcting basin.
Above this surface: the system is in the absorbing basin (under B1) or
the metastable basin (under B1$'$).

The critical cross-substrate redundancy for a given
$(\rS, \rW, \rsub, \dmax, \Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}},
\Lyap_{\mathrm{avg}})$ is:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:critical_redundancy}
(\rhomincross)^* = \frac{\rW \cdot \rsub
  + \dmax \cdot \Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}} / \Lyap_{\mathrm{avg}}}
  {\rS}
\end{equation}
This is the minimum effective cross-substrate redundancy per
safety-relevant dimension needed to stay in the self-correcting basin.
The $\dmax$ term means the threshold is \emph{higher} when subsumption
removes channels covering well-calibrated dimensions (high
$\Delta\rho_{\mathrm{avg}} / \Lyap_{\mathrm{avg}}$).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof proceeds in four steps.  Full details are in
Appendix~\ref{app:proof_strategy}.

\emph{Step 1: Part~(a).}
From Proposition~\ref{prop:error_dynamics}, the per-step bound is an
affine recursion:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:affine_recursion}
\Lyap_{t+1} \leq a_t \cdot \Lyap_t + b_t
\end{equation}
where $a_t = 1 - \rS(t) \cdot \rhomincross(P_t)
  + \nsub(t) \cdot \rW(t)$
and $b_t = \nsub(t) \cdot \dmax \cdot \Delta\rho(t)$, with
$\rS(t) = \alpha(t) \cdot c_{\mathrm{S}}$ and
$\rW(t) = \beta(t) \cdot c_{\mathrm{D}}$.

We require a uniform contraction condition: there exist constants
$\bar{a} \in (0,1)$ and $\bar{b} \geq 0$ such that, for all
sufficiently long windows $[t_0, t_0+T]$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:uniform_contraction}
\frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=t_0}^{t_0+T-1} \ln a_t \leq \ln \bar{a} < 0
\qquad \text{and} \qquad
\frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=t_0}^{t_0+T-1} b_t \leq \bar{b}
\end{equation}
The first condition states that the time-averaged log-contraction rate
is strictly negative (amortized over both subsumption and
non-subsumption steps).  The second bounds the time-averaged additive
drift.

Note that $a_t > 0$ for all $t$: on non-subsumption steps,
$a_t = 1 - \rS(t) \cdot \rhomincross(P_t) > 0$ by the step-size
condition (Section~\ref{sec:lyapunov}); on subsumption steps,
$a_t = 1 - \rS(t) \cdot \rhomincross(P_t) + \rW(t)
\geq 1 - \rS(t) \cdot \rhomincross(P_t) > 0$ since $\rW(t) \geq 0$.
Therefore $\ln a_t$ is well-defined.

The uniform contraction
condition~\eqref{eq:uniform_contraction} is the working hypothesis of
Part~(a); the cumulative error budget $B(t_0,T) < 0$ from the
Simplified Sufficient Condition (below) implies it in the linearized
regime where $\Lyap(\Wm_t)$ is approximately constant over the
window.  The connection: in the linearized regime,
$a_t \approx 1 + (a_t - 1)$ with $|a_t - 1| \ll 1$, so
$\ln a_t \approx a_t - 1$ and the log-averaged contraction rate
equals the arithmetic average $\frac{1}{T}\sum (a_t - 1)$, which is
precisely the unweighted analogue of $B / (T \cdot \Lyap)$.  The
bounded-drift condition on $\bar{b}$ follows from boundedness of
$\Delta\rho(t)$ per step.

Under conditions~\eqref{eq:uniform_contraction}, iterating the affine
recursion~\eqref{eq:affine_recursion} gives (cf.\
\citet{khalil2002nonlinear}, Chapter~4, and
\citet{liberzon2003switching}, Theorem~3.1 on average dwell-time
stability for switched affine systems):
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:iterated_convergence}
\limsup_{t \to \infty} \Lyap(\Wm_t)
  \leq \frac{\bar{b}}{1 - \bar{a}}
\end{equation}
The abstract bound $\bar{b}/(1 - \bar{a})$ depends on the
trajectory-specific constants $\bar{a}$ and $\bar{b}$.  In the
linearized regime (Remark~\ref{rem:linearized}), where $a_t \approx
1 + (a_t - 1)$ and $b_t$ is approximately time-homogeneous, these
evaluate to $\bar{b} = \rsub \cdot \dmax \cdot
\overline{\Delta\rho}$ and $1 - \bar{a} = \rS \cdot \rhomincross -
\rW \cdot \rsub$, yielding Equation~\ref{eq:neighborhood}.  Outside
the linearized regime, $\bar{a}$ and $\bar{b}$ are defined implicitly
by the log-averaging condition~\eqref{eq:uniform_contraction} and may
differ from these closed-form expressions.  The convergence is
geometric in the amortized sense: $\Lyap_t \leq \bar{a}^t \cdot
\Lyap_0 + \bar{b} / (1 - \bar{a})$.

Once $\Lyap(\Wm_t) < \epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}}$, the actor's decisions
are correct and the anti-monopolar property applies.  Define:
\begin{equation*}
\epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}} \coloneqq \sup\!\left\{
  \epsilon > 0 \;:\;
  \forall\, \Wm \text{ with } \Lyap(\Wm) \leq \epsilon,\;
  \pi^*(\Wm) = \pi^*(\Wm^*)
\right\}
\end{equation*}
where $\pi^*(\Wm) = \arg\max_\pi \Vdisc(\pi, \Wm)$ is the locally
rational policy.  Since the action set is finite
(Definition~\ref{def:transition}) and each action gap $G_j(\Wm)$ is
continuous with $G_j(\Wm^*) > 0$ for the optimal action, the set
$\{\Wm : \pi^*(\Wm) = \pi^*(\Wm^*)\}$ is open and contains $\Wm^*$,
so $\epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}} > 0$.

A computable \emph{sufficient condition} for $\Lyap <
\epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}}$ uses the per-dimension cascade thresholds.
Define $\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k} \coloneqq \min_{j \in
\mathcal{A}_t} \epsilon_{\mathrm{crit}}^{(j,k)}$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:cascade}, minimized over all agents; the smallest
error on dimension $k$ that could flip any single pairwise
comparison).  Then $\epsilon_{\mathrm{safe}} \geq \min_k \, w_k \cdot
(\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k})^2$: for any $\Wm$ with
$\Lyap(\Wm) < \min_k w_k \cdot \epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k}^2$, we
have $\epsilon_k < \epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k}$ for every $k$ (since
$\Lyap \geq w_k \cdot \epsilon_k^2$), and by definition of
$\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k}$ no single-coordinate perturbation flips
any pairwise action gap.  Since additionally the Lyapunov ball
$\{\Wm : \Lyap(\Wm) \leq \epsilon\}$ is contained in the open
correct-policy region for sufficiently small $\epsilon$ (by the
openness established above), the per-coordinate bound provides a
sufficient---if conservative---estimate.  (A tighter bound using
$\min_j G_j(\Wm^*) / \|\nabla G_j\|$ would account for joint
multi-coordinate perturbations but requires gradient-norm estimates
we do not derive here.)

\emph{Step 2: Part~(b).}
Suppose $B(t_0, T) > 0$ on some window and
$\rhocross_k(P_{t_0 + T}) = 0$ for some dimension $k$ with
$w_k > 0$.  By Assumption~B1, $\epsilon_k(t)$ is non-decreasing for
$t > t_0 + T$.  If $\epsilon_k$ exceeds $\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit},k}$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:cascade}), the locally
rational action under $\Wm_t$ is subsumption of agents whose channels
cover other dimensions $j \neq k$, driving $\rhocross_j$ toward $0$
and triggering the same logic on dimension $j$.  The cascade
(Appendix~\ref{app:cascade}) drives the system into the neighborhood
of $S^*$; once there, Proposition~\ref{prop:absorbing} ensures the
trajectory stays.

\emph{Step 3: Part~(b$'$).}
Under B1$'$, the indirect signal $g_k$ provides correction at rate
$I_k$ on the blinded dimension.  The cascade structure is the same as
Part~(b) but convergence to $S^*$ is slowed by $I_k$.  The metastable
lifetime $\tau_{\mathrm{meta}} \geq (\delta_{\rext})^2 / (2 I_k)$
emerges from the Cram\'er--Rao-type bound on sequential estimation:
reducing the mean-squared error of $\hat{\rext}$ from
$\delta_{\rext}^2$ to the level at which re-introduction becomes
locally rational requires at least $\delta_{\rext}^2 / (2 I_k)$
steps from a channel providing $I_k$ bits per step.

\emph{Step 4: Part~(c).}
The critical surface is obtained by setting $B(t_0, T) = 0$ in the
linearized approximation
(Equation~\ref{eq:linearized}).
Solving for $\rhomincross$ yields
Equation~\ref{eq:critical_redundancy}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Uniqueness]
We do not claim $S^*$ is the unique absorbing fixed point.  Other fixed
points may exist (partial monopolarity with $\meff = 1$ on a subset of
dimensions).  Part~(b) claims convergence to \emph{a} monopolar fixed
point satisfying Definition~\ref{def:monopolar}, not to a unique one.
Characterizing the full set of absorbing states is an open problem.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Dependence on cross-substrate structure}

\begin{corollary}[Robustness to Same-Substrate Correlation]
\label{cor:same_substrate}
Since Theorem~\ref{thm:phase_boundary} is stated with $\rhomincross$
(cross-substrate redundancy only), its guarantee holds even under
worst-case correlation among same-substrate channels.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Same-substrate channels may share failure modes (both rooted in
silicon, both consulting the same training data).  Cross-substrate
channels satisfy Paper~5's channel isolation property unconditionally:
corruption on one substrate cannot propagate to channels rooted in
another substrate.  Under the additional joint-factorization assumption
from Paper~5 (termed ``joint sufficiency'' there), cross-substrate
channels achieve full conditional independence, making their
correction signals additive.

The modified condition asks: ``do the isolated (and, under joint
factorization, independent) cross-substrate channels alone suffice?''
If yes, the self-correction guarantee holds regardless of
same-substrate correlation.

A tighter bound using all-channel $\rho_{\min}$ (with appropriate
correlation discounting) would yield a larger self-correcting basin.
But the cross-substrate formulation provides a \emph{structural}
guarantee that does not depend on empirically verifying same-substrate
independence.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[$m = 2$ specialization]
\label{rem:m2}
For any substrate partition with $m = 2$, each dimension has at most
one cross-substrate channel per pair.  The design criterion reduces to:
every safety-relevant dimension of $\Wm$ must have a channel on
\emph{both} substrates.  In the common case (biology $+$ silicon), this
means every dimension needs at least one biological observation channel.

This framing should not be read as ``biological substrates are
special.''  Rather, $m = 2$ is a fragile minimum.  Any loss of a single
channel on a single dimension is immediately fatal because there is zero
redundancy margin.  The structural implication is that $m \geq 3$ is
where the redundancy criterion becomes non-trivially satisfiable.

The substrate partition is at the level of abstraction where failure
correlations matter.  Two silicon deployments with uncorrelated failure
modes may functionally constitute different substrates, increasing
effective $m$ beyond the nominal substrate-type count.
\end{remark}

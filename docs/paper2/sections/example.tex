\section{Worked Example}
\label{sec:example}

We demonstrate the poset construction on a population of three agents with
seven capabilities, with all numbers computed explicitly. The computation is
reproduced by \texttt{compute\_example.py} (Appendix~\ref{app:code}).

\subsection{Setup}

Three agents: $a_1$ (runner), $a_2$ (programmer), $a_3$ (musician).

\paragraph{Capabilities and benchmarks.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llrrl@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Capability $d$} & \textbf{Agent(s)} & $\smax$ & $w(d)$ & \textbf{Subsumes} \\
\midrule
Run a mile        & $a_1$         & 600       & 9.23  & --- \\
Run a marathon    & $a_1$         & 21600     & 14.40 & mile \\
Write a function  & $a_2$         & 100       & 6.66  & --- \\
Build a web app   & $a_2$         & 1000      & 9.97  & function \\
Play scales       & $a_3$         & 50        & 5.67  & --- \\
Perform a concert & $a_3$         & 1000      & 9.97  & scales \\
Build a music app & $a_2, a_3$    & 2000000   & 20.93 & webapp, perform \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The subsumption ordering follows the natural prerequisite structure. The
individual chains are straightforward: running a marathon requires running a
mile; building a web app requires writing functions; performing a concert
requires playing scales. In each chain, the supersumer has a richer benchmark.

The cooperative ``build a music app'' forms a diamond in the poset: it
subsumes both ``build a web app'' and ``perform a concert.'' By
Lemma~\ref{lem:nonneg_iw}, its benchmark must satisfy $w(\text{musicapp}) \geq
w(\text{webapp}) + w(\text{perform}) \approx 19.93$, requiring $\smax \geq
1{,}002{,}000$. We use $\smax = 2{,}000{,}000$, reflecting that evaluating a
cooperative product (an application with both technical and musical quality
dimensions) has combinatorial measurement depth: the benchmark must
distinguish quality along both the software and the music axes simultaneously.

\subsection{Computing Independent Weights}

Processing the poset in topological order, applying
Definition~\ref{def:independent_weight} (M\"{o}bius inversion):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrl@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Capability} & $\iw(d)$ & $\sum_{d' \leq d} \mu(d', d) \cdot w(d')$ \\
\midrule
Run a mile        & 9.23 & $+w(\text{mile}) = 9.23$ \\
Write a function  & 6.66 & $+w(\text{function}) = 6.66$ \\
Play scales       & 5.67 & $+w(\text{scales}) = 5.67$ \\
Run a marathon    & 5.17 & $+w(\text{marathon}) - w(\text{mile}) = 14.40 - 9.23$ \\
Perform a concert & 4.29 & $+w(\text{perform}) - w(\text{scales}) = 9.97 - 5.67$ \\
Build a web app   & 3.31 & $+w(\text{webapp}) - w(\text{function}) = 9.97 - 6.66$ \\
Build a music app & 1.00 & $+w(\text{musicapp}) - w(\text{webapp}) - w(\text{perform}) = 20.93 - 9.97 - 9.97$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

All independent weights are non-negative. For chain-structured capabilities
(marathon, concert, webapp), the M\"{o}bius function has $\mu(d', d) = -1$ for
the unique cover and $+1$ for $d$ itself, reducing to subtraction of the
cover's full weight. For the diamond node (musicapp), the M\"{o}bius function
gives $\mu(\text{webapp}, \text{musicapp}) = -1$ and $\mu(\text{perform},
\text{musicapp}) = -1$, but $\mu(\text{function}, \text{musicapp}) = 0$ and
$\mu(\text{scales}, \text{musicapp}) = 0$: the atoms below the covers cancel
out in the inclusion-exclusion and do not appear in the sum.

\subsection{Computing $\volL(G)$}

The full population's joint goal space $G$ contains all seven capabilities.
Since $G$ is the entire poset, $\dc G = G$:
\[
\volL(G) = \sum_{d \in G} \iw(d)
= 9.23 + 6.66 + 5.67 + 5.17 + 4.29 + 3.31 + 1.00
= 35.33
\]

The individual agents' capability sets, computed separately:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrl@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Agent} & $\volL(\Gind{k})$ & \textbf{Capabilities} \\
\midrule
$a_1$ (runner)     & 14.40 & mile, marathon \\
$a_2$ (programmer) &  9.97 & function, webapp \\
$a_3$ (musician)   &  9.97 & scales, perform \\
\midrule
$\sum_k \volL(\Gind{k})$ & 34.33 & \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The individual sets are pairwise poset-disjoint, so $\volL(\bigcup_k
\Gind{k}) = 34.33$ by axiom M4. The cooperative music app contributes
$\volL(G) - 34.33 = 1.00 = \iw(\text{musicapp})$, confirming axiom M6 with
strict inequality.

\subsection{Self-Balancing Demonstration}

\paragraph{Action 1: Eliminate $a_3$ (destruction).} Removing the musician
eliminates three capabilities from $G$: play scales ($\iw = 5.67$), perform a
concert ($\iw = 4.29$), and the cooperative music app ($\iw = 1.00$). The
volume change is:
\[
\Delta \volL = -(5.67 + 4.29 + 1.00) = -10.96
\]
This is strongly contracting. The loss exceeds $a_3$'s individual volume
($9.97$) because the cooperative music app is also destroyed, illustrating the
super-linear elimination cost from Corollary~1.1 of \citet{lasser2026gfm}:
$10.96 > 9.97$.

\paragraph{Action 2: Expand $a_1$'s capabilities (expansion).} Teaching $a_1$
a new capability ``swim'' ($\smax = 300$, $\iw = 8.23$) that was not
previously in $G$:
\[
\Delta \volL = +8.23
\]
This is expanding. The new atom adds its full weight because it is
poset-disjoint from everything in $G$.

\paragraph{Action 3: Restrict $a_2$ (coercion).} Removing $a_2$'s access to
``build a web app'' also eliminates the cooperative ``music app'' (which
subsumes webapp). Volume change:
\[
\Delta \volL = -(3.31 + 1.00) = -4.31
\]
This is contracting, and the contraction exceeds the individual capability lost
($3.31$) because the cooperative dependency amplifies the restriction. This
illustrates Lemma~2 of \citet{lasser2026gfm}: coercion contracts $\vol(G)$
through both direct and cooperative channels.

\paragraph{Summary.} The $\volL$-maximizing actor avoids Actions 1 and 3 and
prefers Action 2. Destruction and coercion are penalized; expansion is
rewarded. All three decisions follow from the same measure without separate
safety mechanisms.

\subsection{Leverage Demonstration}

Suppose the actor has observed the population over several time steps and
estimated leverage for each capability. The musician $a_3$ teaches scales to
other agents, expanding their capability sets; the programmer $a_2$ builds
tools that enable new cooperative capabilities. Their estimated leverages:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrl@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Capability} & $\hat{\lambda}$ & \textbf{Rationale} \\
\midrule
Run a mile        & 0.0 & static; does not accelerate $\volL$ growth \\
Run a marathon    & 0.0 & static \\
Write a function  & 0.5 & enables building other capabilities \\
Build a web app   & 1.0 & creates cooperative infrastructure \\
Play scales       & 0.8 & teaches others; expands individual sets \\
Perform a concert & 0.3 & modest cooperative contribution \\
Build a music app & 2.0 & cooperative output that enables further apps \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

With $\alpha = 1$, the leverage bonus $\alpha \hat{\lambda}(d) \cdot \iw(d)$
for each capability is:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrrrr@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Capability} & $\iw(d)$ & $\hat{\lambda}$ & Leverage bonus & Total score contribution \\
\midrule
Run a mile        & 9.23 & 0.0 & 0.00 & 9.23 \\
Run a marathon    & 5.17 & 0.0 & 0.00 & 5.17 \\
Write a function  & 6.66 & 0.5 & 3.33 & 9.99 \\
Build a web app   & 3.31 & 1.0 & 3.31 & 6.62 \\
Play scales       & 5.67 & 0.8 & 4.54 & 10.21 \\
Perform a concert & 4.29 & 0.3 & 1.29 & 5.58 \\
Build a music app & 1.00 & 2.0 & 2.00 & 3.00 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The score contribution column ($\iw(d) + \alpha \hat{\lambda}(d) \cdot
\iw(d)$) shows how leverage reshapes the actor's investment priorities. Under
the base measure alone, ``run a mile'' ($\iw = 9.23$) dominates ``play
scales'' ($\iw = 5.67$). Under the leverage-adjusted score, ``play scales''
(total $= 10.21$) overtakes ``run a mile'' (total $= 9.23$) because the
musician's teaching capability accelerates $\volL$ growth for the whole
population. An action that expands ``play scales'' for a new agent scores
higher than an action that expands ``run a mile,'' even though the base
volume contribution is smaller. This illustrates
Proposition~\ref{prop:leverage_cooperation}: cooperation with high-leverage
agents is disproportionately rewarded in the action-selection score.

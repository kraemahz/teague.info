\begin{abstract}
Goal-frontier maximization (GFM) aligns an agent by maximizing $\vol(G)$, the
    volume of the jointly achievable capability space across all agents. The
    foundational paper \citep{lasser2026gfm} proved structural alignment
    properties conditional on a measure satisfying certain axioms, but left the
    capability space abstract and the measure intractable. We construct a
    concrete, computable capability space as a weighted lattice discovered
    through dual-channel discovery: agent communication and behavioral
    observation. Capabilities are ordered by subsumption and weighted by the
    information content of their benchmarks. We define a lattice measure
    $\volL$ via M\"{o}bius inversion, prove it satisfies the required axioms,
    and show that $\volL(G)$ is computable in polynomial time on finite
    lattices. The lattice construction also upgrades the local volume estimator
    from ternary sign estimation to real-valued magnitude estimation, resolving
    an acknowledged limitation of the original framework. We demonstrate the
    construction with a worked example showing self-balancing decisions on a
    concrete lattice of benchmarked capabilities.
\end{abstract}

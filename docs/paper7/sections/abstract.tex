\begin{abstract}
A restriction justified by a long-timeline risk cannot be revised
through observation: the evidence that would revise it cannot
arrive within any practical decision cycle, and waiting for the
catastrophe that would generate the evidence is the failure mode
the restriction was meant to prevent.  We call this the
\emph{Wamura problem}.  We formalize the structural asymmetry,
prove that max-aggregation with neutral-prior initialization
produces monotone overcaution absent external intervention, and
introduce \emph{controlled relaxation protocols}: bounded,
monitored exercise windows that generate genuine Bayesian evidence
about risk claims while provably bounding maximum damage.  A
scope-construction theorem bounds the damage from a test by a
user-set parameter $\damagebound$ even when cross-boundary
cooperative capabilities could propagate the test's effects.  A
convergence theorem shows that repeated controlled relaxation
drives risk estimates almost surely to truth at exponential rate,
with the rate given by the Kullback--Leibler divergence between
the observation distributions.  We extend the protocol to
\emph{keystone-refinement tests}, which handle the common case
where a restriction is not obsolete but misconfigured: the
underlying risk pathway still operates, but technological
progress has made narrower keystones available that would block
it without blocking adjacent benign pathways.  For the subset of risks admitting
bounded-scope feasibility (a precondition we delineate in
Section~\ref{sec:discussion}), the protocol closes the most
important open problem named
in~\citep{lasser2026horizon} and resolves the
degenerate-phase-boundary case
of~\citep[Proposition~4]{lasser2026phase}.
\end{abstract}

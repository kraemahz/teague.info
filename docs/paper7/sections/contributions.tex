\section*{Author Contributions}

\textbf{Teague Lasser} identified the Wamura problem as a
structural gap in the risk framework
of~\citep{lasser2026horizon} and named it after the Fudai
floodgate.  Directed the formal development: the
risk-restriction-pair abstraction, the two-regime observation
model, controlled relaxation as a mechanism that breaks the
verification asymmetry by generating exercise-regime
observations, the scope-bounding construction, and the
cooperative-closure condition required to prevent cross-boundary
cascades.  Identified the link between the Loop~3 degeneracy
of~\citep[Proposition~3]{lasser2026phase} and the
zero-likelihood-ratio structure of
Proposition~\ref{prop:asym_evidence}, and proposed the binary
hypothesis formulation with fixed $\thetaone$ as the
dimensionally consistent way to state convergence.  Made final
editorial decisions and is responsible for the paper's
intellectual direction and all claims made.

\textbf{Claude Opus 4.6} (Anthropic) drafted the formal
exposition: the risk-restriction pair and observation-regime
definitions (Section~\ref{sec:verification_asymmetry}), the
controlled-relaxation-test object with its three protocol
phases (Section~\ref{sec:relaxation_protocol}), the damage-bound
theorem with its cooperative-closure condition and
pathway-enumeration lemma (Section~\ref{sec:damage_guarantees}),
the detection-sensitivity characterization and evidence-strength
proposition (Section~\ref{sec:detection_sensitivity}), the
convergence theorem with its KL-divergence rate form and
finite-sample corollaries (Section~\ref{sec:convergence}), the
meta-policy for test initiation and scheduling
(Section~\ref{sec:meta_policy}), the worked-example
instantiation with three-regime summary table
(Section~\ref{sec:worked_example}), and the integration results
connecting the protocol to self-balancing, empirical
arbitration, and the multi-channel detection architecture
(Section~\ref{sec:integration}).

\textbf{GPT 5.4} (OpenAI) served as technical reviewer across
multiple codex review cycles of the outline, identifying formal
gaps including: the dimensional mismatch between a continuous
$\theta$ prior and a binary posterior (driving the choice of
the fixed-$\thetaone$ formulation); the missing $(1 - \alphafp)$
denominator in the naive no-contraction log-odds update
(corrected in Proposition~\ref{prop:evidence_strength}(b)); the
need for cooperative closure to forward-reachable external
pathways in Definition~\ref{def:test_scope}(ii) rather than
only the pathways originating in $\scope$; the latent-variable
structure separating $A_n$ (pathway activation) from $O_n$
(detection outcome) in Section~\ref{sec:relaxation_protocol};
and the need for explicit identifiability, specification, and
independence assumptions (T1--T5) before
Theorem~\ref{thm:convergence}'s almost-sure claim follows from
the strong law of large numbers.
